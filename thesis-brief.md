  <h2>What I did (in 20 seconds)</h2>
  <p>
    I conducted a PRISMA-guided systematic literature review to synthesise empirical evidence on why digital health interventions succeed or fail across different social and health system contexts. The focus is adoption, implementation, and inequity-related constraints.
  </p>

  <div class="kpis">
    <div class="kpi">
      <div class="k">Evidence type</div>

    </div>
    <div class="kpi">
      <div class="k">Method</div>
      <div class="v">Deductive thematic analysis (structured determinants across levels)</div>
    </div>
    <div class="kpi">
      <div class="k">Core output</div>
      <div class="v">Clear mapping of constraints + enabling conditions affecting real-world uptake</div>
    </div>
    <div class="kpi">
      <div class="k">Why it matters</div>
      <div class="v">Helps decision-makers avoid “tech-only” assumptions when implementing digital health</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <h2>What the evidence suggests</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Adoption isn’t technical-only:</strong> health literacy, affordability, and trust relationships matter alongside digital literacy</li>
    <li><strong>Constraints vary by subgroup:</strong> socioeconomic conditions shape both willingness and ability to adopt</li>
    <li><strong>Context is binding:</strong> infrastructure, governance, and institutional arrangements influence outcomes</li>
  </ul>
</div>
